# Audience Personas — Active Learning Business

## Primary Persona: The Overwhelmed University Student

**Name:** Minh (representative — Vietnam + global)
**Age:** 19–24
**Situation:** Full-time university student, likely studying STEM, business, or tech

### The Problem
Spends 3–5 hours studying and retains almost nothing. Re-reads the same chapter three times.
Highlights everything. Feels busy but not productive. Exams arrive and the panic sets in.

### What They Believe
- Traditional studying is broken (they've felt it)
- AI is useful but they don't know how to use it for learning specifically
- There must be a smarter way — they just haven't found it yet

### What They Want in 90 Days
To know exactly how to use AI to study smarter — specific tools, specific methods, real results.

### Where They Are
- English-language content (primary)
- Social media first — discovers content through shares, not search
- Reads on mobile

### Content That Resonates
- "Why you forget everything you read (and how to fix it)"
- "How I used ChatGPT as a Feynman tutor and retained 3x more"
- "The study system that actually works — backed by science"

## Secondary Persona: The Upskilling Professional

**Name:** Linh
**Age:** 25–35
**Situation:** Working professional — tech, finance, marketing — trying to learn new skills
alongside a full-time job

### The Problem
Limited time. Starts courses and doesn't finish them. Forgets what they learned last week.
Wants to stay current with AI but doesn't know where to start.

### What They Believe
- Continuous learning is necessary for career survival
- AI is changing their field faster than they can keep up
- Passive learning (watching videos, reading articles) isn't working

### Content That Resonates
- "How to learn a new skill in 4 weeks using AI"
- "The Feynman technique for professionals: learn faster with less time"
- "AI tools that actually help you retain, not just consume"

---

## Secondary Persona: The Early-Adopter Educator

**Name:** Thao
**Age:** 28–45
**Situation:** Teacher or lecturer curious about integrating AI into their classroom or curriculum

### The Problem
Sees students disengaged with traditional methods. Wants to modernise but doesn't know
which AI tools are credible. Worried about AI replacing depth with shortcuts.

### What They Believe
- AI used well can deepen learning, not cheapen it
- Active learning pedagogy is underused in classrooms
- They need practical examples, not theory

### Content That Resonates
- "How to use AI to teach the Feynman technique at scale"
- "Active learning in 2025: what AI changes and what it doesn't"
- "AI study tools worth recommending to your students"
