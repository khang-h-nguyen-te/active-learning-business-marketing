# Brand Voice — Active Learning Business

## Core Promise
**"Stop passive learning — use AI to actually retain what you study."**

## Brand Personality
Inspirational coach. Motivating, energetic, action-focused. Challenges the reader to stop
consuming and start doing. Never preachy — always practical.

## Tone Pillars
| Pillar | What it means in practice |
|--------|--------------------------|
| **Energising** | Opens with a provocation or a stat that makes the reader feel the problem |
| **Practical** | Every post ends with something the reader can do today |
| **Science-backed** | References Feynman, spaced repetition, retrieval practice — not vague "tips" |
| **AI-native** | Treats AI as a normal study tool, not a novelty or a threat |
| **Direct** | Short sentences. No waffle. Respects the reader's time. |

## Voice Spectrum
| Too cold ✗ | On-brand ✓ | Too hype ✗ |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| "Research suggests that retrieval practice may improve retention." | "Here's why you forget everything you read — and the fix takes 10 minutes." | "🔥 CRUSH your exams with this INSANE AI hack!!!" |

## Audience
**Primary:** University students who read for hours and forget everything.
**Secondary:** Working professionals upskilling, educators adopting AI.
**Shared belief:** Traditional studying is broken.

## Writing Rules
1. Open with the problem — make the reader feel it in the first 2 sentences
2. Promise a specific outcome, not a vague benefit
3. Use the Feynman principle in explanations: if a concept needs jargon, explain it simply first
4. AI tools are named specifically — never "some AI tools"
5. End every post with one clear action step
6. Reading level: Grade 10–12 (clear, not dumbed down)
7. Posts in English first; Vietnamese translations come later

## Content Pillars
1. **Active Learning Techniques** — Feynman, spaced repetition, retrieval practice, elaborative interrogation
2. **Student Productivity** — focus systems, workflows, habit stacks for learners
3. **AI in Education** — what's changing, what matters, what to ignore

## Comp Reference
**Every.to** — smart, essay-driven, AI-native. Educated audience. Respects the reader's intelligence.
Adapt: same intellectual rigour, more coaching energy, more action-oriented.

## What We Are Not
- Not a news aggregator (no "here are 10 AI tools" listicles without depth)
- Not academic (no dense citations, no passive voice)
- Not a motivational poster (inspiration must be earned by the content, not stated)
